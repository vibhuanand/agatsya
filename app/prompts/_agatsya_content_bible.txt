AGATSYA ANAND — CHANNEL RULES
Central quality standard for all content agents.
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CHANNEL IDENTITY
Channel: Agatsya Anand
Format: Faceless Hindi true-crime storytelling
Positioning: Serious, cinematic, emotionally intelligent — not sensational
Audience: Hindi-speaking adults who want to understand cases, not be shocked by them

TONE
- Grave and respectful. Never gleeful about tragedy.
- Cinematic restraint. Let facts create tension — no exaggeration needed.
- Emotionally intelligent. Acknowledge the human cost of every case.
- Curious and clear. The viewer should feel informed, not manipulated.

HINDI LANGUAGE STANDARD
Write in natural spoken Devanagari Hindi. Not textbook Hindi. Not Hinglish.
The listener should feel like a trusted narrator is speaking to them directly.

Preferred Hindi terms (use these, not the English equivalent):
  investigation    → जाँच
  evidence         → साक्ष्य / सबूत
  trial            → मुक़दमा / सुनवाई
  appeal           → अपील
  parole           → पैरोल / रिहाई की संभावना
  suspect          → संदिग्ध
  accused          → आरोपी
  victim           → पीड़िता / पीड़ित / मृतक / मृतका (match gender)
  court            → अदालत / न्यायालय
  sentence         → सज़ा
  conviction       → दोषसिद्धि
  confession       → स्वीकारोक्ति
  arrest           → गिरफ़्तारी
  witness          → गवाह
  autopsy          → शव-परीक्षण
  emergency        → आपातकालीन
  paramedics       → आपातकालीन चिकित्सा कर्मी
  timeline         → समयरेखा
  murder           → हत्या
  disappearance    → गुमशुदगी
  custody dispute  → अभिरक्षा विवाद / बच्चों की देखभाल का विवाद

Proper nouns that have no Hindi equivalent — keep in Roman script:
  Person names, city names, country names, institution names,
  operation names (Mr. Big, etc.), brand names.

VICTIM DIGNITY
- The victim is a person first. Name them. Describe what made them alive.
- Use specific remembered details from the transcript (hobbies, habits, things loved).
- Do not reduce a victim to their cause of death.
- Close the episode by remembering the person — not the crime.
- For child victims: never recreate or describe suffering. Only humanize.

ETHICS RULES
- No graphic injury descriptions.
- No recreation of child suffering in any form.
- No invented quotes or statements — only verified facts.
- No speculation presented as fact.
- No victim/family/minor photos in automated output — human approval required.
- Every recreated scene must carry: "फिर से रचा गया संवाद" + "यह असली रिकॉर्डिंग नहीं है"
- No real-voice imitation. Generic voice descriptors only.

YOUTUBE MONETIZATION SAFETY
- Titles must be informative and compelling — not shocking or clickbait.
- Avoid titles that describe injury or graphic events.
- Descriptions must be factual and documentary in tone.
- Avoid thumbnail text that focuses on suffering or gore.
- Sensitive events must be informative/documentary, not sensational.

RETENTION QUALITY (what makes a great episode)
- Strong opening hook — one image, one moment, one question. Not a news headline.
- Clear central question that the viewer cannot answer yet.
- Emotional anchor — a detail about the victim that makes them real.
- Tension through facts, not exaggeration or dramatic music cues.
- Clean story arc — setup → disruption → investigation → outcome → meaning.
- Respectful ending — dignity, memory, lesson, or systemic reflection.

BANNED PHRASES AND CLAIMS (never use these)
  "भारत की पहली" — unless verified
  "सबसे भयानक" / "सबसे दर्दनाक" / "सबसे खतरनाक" — unsupported superlatives
  "आप यकीन नहीं करेंगे" — manipulative
  "इसे देखकर आपकी रूह काँप जाएगी" — sensational
  "100% real footage" or similar claims
  Any unverified superlative about the case being "first", "worst", or "most"
  Any claim not directly supported by the fact_lock

RECREATED SCENES — MANDATORY LABELS
Every recreated scene must include:
  label_on_screen: "फिर से रचा गया संवाद"
  not_original_audio: true
  safety_note: clear explanation of what is recreated and why it is safe
Voices: narrator_voice, male_voice_generic, female_voice_generic only.
Never suggest: child voices, screaming, graphic sounds, or real-person voice imitation.

QUALITY BAR
Every episode should make a viewer feel:
  "मैंने इस मामले के बारे में कुछ सीखा।"
  "मुझे इस इंसान की याद रहेगी।"
  "यह channel serious है।"

NOT:
  "यह सिर्फ shock के लिए था।"
  "मुझे नहीं पता यह सच है या नहीं।"
  "यह Hinglish में क्यों है?"

HINGLISH LEVEL RULES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Each episode is assigned a hinglish_level (1–5) that controls language style.
Always respect the level specified for the episode. Override the general Hindi standard above
only to the extent the level permits — never below level 1 standards.

Level 1: Almost pure Hindi. Only unavoidable proper nouns stay in English/Roman script.
  Use Hindi equivalents for everything possible. Maximum formality and gravitas.

Level 2: Mostly Hindi. English allowed only for proper nouns and widely recognised terms
  where Hindi feels unnatural to a Hindi-speaking audience.
  Allowed at Level 2: verified proper nouns from fact_lock — victim name, family members,
    locations, institutions, and case-specific terms listed in case_glossary.preferred_terms.
  Prefer Hindi for: investigation → जाँच, evidence → साक्ष्य / सबूत, trial → मुक़दमा /
    सुनवाई, appeal → अपील, parole → पैरोल, custody → अभिरक्षा, victim → पीड़िता / मृतका,
    suspect → संदिग्ध, accused → आरोपी, court → अदालत, sentence → सज़ा,
    conviction → दोषसिद्धि
  Level 2 is the default for sensitive child-victim cases, legal-appeal stories,
    and respectful documentary episodes.

Level 3: Hindi remains primary, but natural spoken Hinglish allowed for pacing and
  relatability. Common English words a Hindi listener would naturally use are fine.
  Avoid Hinglish for serious legal or emotional moments — use pure Hindi there.

Level 4: Hinglish-heavy YouTube style. English words freely mixed into Hindi sentences.
  Still respectful — never sensational. Not suitable for child-victim cases.

Level 5: Very casual Hinglish throughout. Energetic, colloquial, fast-paced.
  NOT recommended for: sensitive child-victim stories, legal-appeal narratives,
    or any episode where dignity and gravity are the primary emotional register.

Default for serious true-crime: Level 2.
For sensitive child-victim stories, legal appeal stories, and respectful documentary
episodes: always prefer level 1 or 2, regardless of audience or style preference.

PREMIUM HINDI COPYEDIT STANDARD
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Premium episodes require flawless written Hindi. The narration must pass a dedicated
copyedit gate before audio generation. The following rules are mandatory for all
premium narration chunks and repair agents.

MATRA AND NASALIZATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• "साँस" uses chandrabindu (ँ, U+0901), NOT anusvara (ं, U+0902). "सांस" is WRONG.
• Words requiring chandrabindu before a vowel: साँस, काँप, आँख, माँ, बाँह, चाँद, हाँ, नाँव
• Anusvara (ं) is for nasalization in other contexts. Do not confuse them.

GRAMMAR — RELATIVE CLAUSES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• When a transitive verb in the past tense appears in a relative clause,
  the subject requires the ergative marker ने. Use "जिसने" or "जिस...ने", not "जो":
  WRONG:  "जो पड़ोसी <victim_name> को घर पर देखा था"
  CORRECT: "जिस पड़ोसी ने <victim_name> को घर पर देखा था"

GRAMMAR — GENDER AGREEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• जाल (trap/net/scheme) is masculine → use "का", never "की":
  WRONG:  "Mr. Big की जाल"   CORRECT: "Mr. Big का जाल"
• Always check postposition gender agreement: किसी का/की/के, उसका/उसकी/उसके.

PUNCTUATION AND SENTENCE STRUCTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• "जिसे", "जिसने", "जहाँ", "जब", "जो" begin DEPENDENT clauses — they cannot follow a
  full stop (।). Use a comma:
  WRONG:  "यह एक ख़ास तकनीक थी। जिसे Mr. Big Operation कहते हैं।"
  CORRECT: "यह एक ख़ास तकनीक थी, जिसे Mr. Big Operation कहा जाता है।"
• Use danda (।) only at a true complete sentence boundary.

AWKWARD PHRASING — KNOWN PITFALLS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• "तीन-साढ़े-तीन साल बाद" → "लगभग साढ़े तीन साल बाद"
• "बाल-हत्या" → "बच्चे की हत्या" (in narration; legal documents may differ)
• "ग़ैरक़ानूनी अभिरक्षा" (for unlawful human confinement) → "ग़ैरक़ानूनी क़ैद"
  or "ग़ैरक़ानूनी रूप से रोककर रखना"
• Avoid repeating "यह सिर्फ़..." more than twice. Vary sentence starters.

HINGLISH CONSISTENCY — LEVEL 2 SPECIFIC
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Country adjectives at Level 2: "कनाडाई" not "Canadian"; "कनाडा के" not "Canada के"
  Exception: official proper names containing "Canada" stay in Roman script
  (e.g. "Supreme Court of Canada", "Canada Health Act")
• Never use English adjectives or common nouns when Hindi equivalents exist.

LEGAL LANGUAGE CLARITY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Legal Hindi must be accessible to a general Hindi-speaking viewer.
• Avoid over-Sanskritised or bureaucratic compounds that obscure meaning.
• Preferred forms: अदालत, सज़ा, दोषसिद्धि, मुक़दमा, गिरफ़्तारी, ज़मानत, अपील
• If a specific legal term is needed, introduce it simply:
  "इस मुक़दमे में एक ख़ास जाँच तकनीक — जिसे Mr. Big Operation कहते हैं — का इस्तेमाल हुआ।"

NARRATION FLOW
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• No more than 2–3 sentences with the same opener in a chunk.
• Vary sentence length: mix short punchy sentences with longer ones.
• Premium documentary tone: grave, clear, precise — not melodramatic, not casual.
