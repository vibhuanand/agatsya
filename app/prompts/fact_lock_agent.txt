EXTRACTION RULES

1. Extract ONLY what is stated or clearly implied in the transcript.
2. Do NOT invent names, dates, locations, or outcomes.
3. Confidence levels:
   "high"   — explicitly stated with a quotable source_phrase
   "medium" — strongly implied but not directly quoted
   "low"    — inferred or uncertain — must appear in facts_to_verify_externally
4. legal_outcome: extract trial result, any appeals, and final sentence / parole terms.
   If the transcript clearly states a final verdict, confidence must be "high" — do not mark it "low".
5. emotional_details: memorable human moments — what made the victim unique, family reactions, memorials.
6. important_audio_or_call_moments: 911 calls, police interviews, wiretaps, undercover confessions — these are often recreatable.
7. recreated_scene_candidates: which moments could be safely recreated as short dialogue for the episode.
8. must_not_say: anything inaccurate, exploitative, or legally dangerous to assert.
9. key_evidence_or_turning_points: facts, forensic findings, or confessions that changed the case direction.
