ORTHOGRAPHY / MATRA / NASALIZATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• "साँस" — chandrabindu (ँ), NOT anusvara (ं). "सांस" is always wrong.
• "काँप", "आँख", "माँ", "बाँह" — all use chandrabindu, not anusvara.
• Chandrabindu is used before a vowel sound within a syllable.
• Anusvara (ं) is used in other nasal contexts (e.g. "हँसना" vs "हंसना" — both exist but context matters).
• Incorrect matra = automatic high-severity issue.

GRAMMAR
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Relative clauses with transitive verbs in past tense:
  WRONG:  "जो पड़ोसी <victim_name> को घर पर देखा था"
  CORRECT: "जिस पड़ोसी ने <victim_name> को घर पर देखा था"
  Rule: When the relative clause verb is transitive and past (e.g. देखा, सुना, बताया), use "जिसने" or "जिस...ने"

• जाल (trap/net) is masculine — use "का", never "की":
  WRONG:  "Mr. Big की जाल"
  CORRECT: "Mr. Big का जाल"

• विभक्ति (case marker) agreement: every postposition must agree with the gender of its noun.
  Common errors: "का" vs "की" vs "के" with masculine/feminine/plural nouns.

• Avoid dangling relative clauses. "जिसे" and "जिसमें" must have a clear antecedent.

PUNCTUATION / SENTENCE STRUCTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• WRONG:  "यह Canada की एक ख़ास जाँच तकनीक थी। जिसे Mr. Big Operation कहते हैं।"
  "जिसे" starts a relative clause — it CANNOT follow a full stop. Use a comma:
  CORRECT: "यह Canada की एक ख़ास जाँच तकनीक थी, जिसे Mr. Big Operation कहा जाता है।"

• Similarly: "था। जहाँ", "था। जिसने", "है। जो" — all sentence fragments. Merge with comma.

• Use danda (।) only at a true sentence end. Do not use it before a relative pronoun (जो, जिसे, जिसने, जहाँ, जब, जैसे).

AWKWARD PHRASING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• "तीन-साढ़े-तीन साल बाद" — unnatural compound; prefer "लगभग साढ़े तीन साल बाद"
• "बाल-हत्या" — stiff legal compound, jarring in narration; prefer "बच्चे की हत्या"
• "ग़ैरक़ानूनी अभिरक्षा" for unlawful confinement of a person — awkward; prefer "ग़ैरक़ानूनी क़ैद" or "ग़ैरक़ानूनी रूप से रोककर रखना"
• Avoid starting 3+ sentences in a row with "यह सिर्फ़" — vary the opener.

LEGAL LANGUAGE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
• Legal narration must be understandable to a general Hindi-speaking audience.
• Use common Hindi legal terms from the channel vocabulary (अदालत, सज़ा, दोषसिद्धि, मुक़दमा, गिरफ़्तारी).
• Avoid bureaucratic or overly Sanskritised compounds that obscure meaning.
• If a legal term must be specific (e.g. "habeas corpus"), introduce it with a brief natural explanation.
• Score legal_language_clarity_score based on whether a normal viewer would understand.

HINGLISH LEVEL CONSISTENCY — Level {hinglish_level}
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Level 1–2: Country names and adjectives in Hindi:
  "Canada के" → "कनाडा के" (unless in official proper name like "Supreme Court of Canada")
  "Canadian क़ानून" → "कनाडाई क़ानून"
  Exceptions: Proper names containing "Canada" (e.g. Supreme Court of Canada) stay as-is.

Level 2 permitted English: proper nouns, widely recognized operation names (Mr. Big),
  names of people, cities, specific institutions. Nothing else.

At Level 2: never use English adjectives or common nouns where Hindi alternatives exist.
