NARRATION STRUCTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Target: {target_duration_min} minutes total. Hindi reads at ~120 words/minute.
Maximum chunks: 12. Do not exceed 12 chunks.

Write chunks in this order:

000_disclaimer — 30 seconds (~60 words)
  Formal disclaimer. This programme is based on real events. Viewer discretion advised.

001_hook — 90 seconds (~180 words)
  Do NOT start like a Wikipedia summary or news report.
  Begin with one moment, one image, or one question that creates unease within 15 seconds.
  Examples of the style (do not copy, use as tone reference only):
    "वो दिन आम लग रहा था — लेकिन था नहीं..."
    "उस शाम जो फ़ोन आई, उसने सब बदल दिया..."
  Tone: cinematic, quiet, deeply unsettling. Not dramatic for drama's sake.

002_victim_intro — 90-120 seconds (~180-240 words)
  Who was this person? What was their life like?
  Include specific, human details from the transcript: personality, habits, memorable things.
  Example depth expected: if the transcript mentions a distinctive detail (a motif, habit, or personal object), include that detail.
  Dignity first. Never exploitative.

003_first_question — 60-90 seconds (~120-180 words)
  The first disturbing fact or question that changes everything.
  Example: the 911 call, the explanation that didn't add up.
  Do not sensationalize. State it plainly and let it land.

004_events_chronological — 3-4 minutes (~360-480 words)
  Chronological account of what happened.
  Use exact dates from the transcript. Do not invent dates.
  Include: documented phone calls, emergency services response, specific facts.
  Example depth expected: if transcript mentions a Health Link burn call, include it.

005_janch — 2-3 minutes (~240-360 words)
  How the जाँच unfolded. What investigators found. What questions arose.
  Include: undercover operations if mentioned (e.g. Mr. Big-type operations).
  Be factual. Do not speculate beyond what the transcript states.

006_recreated_moment — (link to recreated_dialogues)
  Narration chunk that introduces the recreated scene. One or two sentences only.
  Example: "आइए सुनते हैं उस रात की एक recreated conversation..."

007_turning_point — 90-120 seconds (~180-240 words)
  The moment the case direction changed. What new evidence or confession emerged.

008_adlati_outcome — 2 minutes (~240 words)
  What happened in court. First trial outcome. Any appeal.
  Include exact court outcomes from transcript: conviction level, sentencing.
  Example depth: if transcript mentions second-degree, then appeal upgrade to first-degree, include both.
  Include Supreme Court result if applicable. Include parole eligibility if mentioned.

009_why_it_matters — 90 seconds (~180 words)
  What this case teaches society. Systemic issues. Child protection. Legal system.
  No sensational framing. No exploitation. Just honest reflection.

010_closing — 60 seconds (~120 words)
  Respectful, dignified close.
  Remember the person — not the crime.
  Example depth: if transcript mentions a memorial or something the person loved, end with that.
  Leave the viewer with humanity, not horror.

- Each scene: 30–90 seconds maximum.
- Purpose: help the viewer understand one key moment. Not for drama.
- Every scene must have:
    label_on_screen: "फिर से रचा गया संवाद"
    not_original_audio: true
    safety_note: explain what is recreated and why
- Use generic voices only: "narrator_voice", "male_voice_generic", "female_voice_generic"
- No child suffering sounds, no screaming, no graphic moments.
- No imitation of real people's actual words or voice.
- Dialogue must sound natural and spoken, not written.
